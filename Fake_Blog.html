Cenotes in Mexico

What are Cenotes?

A cenote is a natural pit, or sinkhole, that results from the collapse of limestone bedrock
that exposes groundwater underneath. Especially associated with the Yucatan Peninsula of Mexico,
they were also used by the ancient Maya for sacrifical offerings.

Reasons to visit a Cenote:

  • They are very pretty sights, photo worthy!
  • A good place to cool off, you can enjoy a pleasant swim at one.
  • The history of how the Mayans used them is very interesting to hear.
  • You can go diving for a modest price of 80 USD.
